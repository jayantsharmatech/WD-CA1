1. The Starry Night

This iconic oil-on-canvas painting by Vincent van Gogh depicts the view from the east-facing window of his asylum room at Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, just before sunrise, with the addition of an imaginary village. It is *widely regarded* as one of Van Gogh's best works.

Key Details

  • Artist: Vincent van Gogh
  • Year: 1889
  • Medium: Oil on canvas
  • Location: Museum of Modern Art (MoMA), New York

2. Mona Lisa

The Mona Lisa, also known as La Gioconda, is a half-length portrait painting by the Italian Renaissance artist Leonardo da Vinci. It has been described as "the best known, the most visited, the most written about, the most sung about, the most parodied work of art in the world."

3. The Persistence of Memory

This 1931 painting by Salvador Dalí is one of the most recognizable works of Surrealism. It introduced the image of the "soft watches," which are often interpreted as a visual representation of Albert Einstein's theory of relativity and the subjective nature of time. Time is fluid, not rigid.

Key Symbolic Elements

  1. Soft Watches: Represents the fluidity and subjective experience of time.
  2. The Mass: The central, limp, human-like figure in the sand.
  3. Ants: Often symbolizing decay and death in Dalí's work.
  4. Port Lligat Landscape: The background is the coastline of Catalonia, Spain.
